Introduction back to ToC

The purpose of OSLO-STEPS is to describe user journeys as sequences of conditional steps for governmental public services, from the user perspective. As a possible application, a planning-engine is supposed to build plans given the steps to reach a goal. These plans are first optimized and then personalized. The described application is foreseen to present different plans to the user and perform A/B testing of the user interface. Possible use-cases within the frame of the FAST project were discussed in the project consortium, and a use-case was selected. Finding information regarding public services is difficult for citizens, as they often have to interact with multiple eGovernment systems which differ between regions and/or countries. Aligning public services is one objective of the EU Single Market, which stipulates the free movement of goods, services and capital across the EU [CPSV-AP]. Citizens usually interact with the government based on a change in their immediate situation. Such a situation change is described as life events. Several life events were defined on European level [CPSV-AP]. and were used by initiatives such as [OSLO] and [ISAÂ²], to group public services accordingly. Given multiple possible life events, selecting one of it as a use-case was on the agenda of a consortium meeting on the 26th of June 2018. Existing life events, such as "Finding a new job", and "Looking for a new place to live" were discussed and based on a mix of complexity, feasibility and available consortium-resources, the life event "Onboarding in a new municipality" was selected as use-case.
